# MEDDPIC Framework Reference

Comprehensive qualification methodology for complex B2B sales. Use this guide for scoring and discovery questions.

## Framework Elements

### M - Metrics
**Definition**: Quantifiable measures of economic impact the prospect expects from the solution.

**What to Look For**:
- Hard numbers (cost savings, revenue increase, time reduction)
- Current baseline measurements
- Target/desired improvements
- Timeframe for ROI

**Qualification Questions**:
- What metrics are you currently tracking for this initiative?
- What would success look like in measurable terms?
- What is the current cost of the problem?
- What ROI are you expecting from a solution?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: No metrics discussed, vague "improvement" language only
- 5-6: Some metrics mentioned but not quantified or validated
- 7-8: Clear metrics with baseline and targets
- 9-10: Fully documented business case with validated numbers

---

### E - Economic Buyer
**Definition**: The person with final authority to approve budget and sign contracts. They can say YES when everyone else says NO.

**Characteristics**:
- Controls discretionary budget
- Can approve unplanned expenditures
- Often C-level or VP in smaller deals
- May delegate evaluation but retains final say

**Qualification Questions**:
- Who has final sign-off authority for this investment?
- Who controls the budget for this initiative?
- Have you made purchases of this size before? Who approved?
- Is there anyone else who needs to approve?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Economic Buyer unknown or no access
- 5-6: Identified but no direct engagement
- 7-8: Engaged and positive, understands value
- 9-10: Committed champion with budget confirmed

---

### D - Decision Criteria
**Definition**: The formal and informal factors the prospect will use to evaluate and compare solutions.

**Types**:
- **Technical**: Features, integrations, performance, security
- **Business**: ROI, TCO, vendor stability, support
- **Personal**: Ease of use, career impact, risk

**Qualification Questions**:
- What are your must-have requirements?
- How will you evaluate different solutions?
- What would make you choose one vendor over another?
- Are there any deal-breakers we should know about?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Unknown criteria or purely price-driven
- 5-6: Some criteria known, unclear weighting
- 7-8: Clear criteria documented, we match well
- 9-10: We helped define criteria, strong alignment

---

### D - Decision Process
**Definition**: The series of steps, approvals, and evaluations the prospect will follow to make a purchase decision.

**Key Elements**:
- Evaluation stages and timeline
- People involved at each stage
- Technical validation requirements (POC, pilot)
- Internal approval workflow

**Qualification Questions**:
- Walk me through how you've made similar decisions before?
- What steps remain before you can move forward?
- Who else needs to be involved in this evaluation?
- What is your target timeline for a decision?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Process unknown or undefined
- 5-6: General steps known, timeline unclear
- 7-8: Clear process mapped with timeline
- 9-10: Process agreed, milestones scheduled

---

### P - Paper Process
**Definition**: The administrative and legal steps required to execute a contract after the decision is made.

**Key Elements**:
- Procurement requirements (RFP, vendor registration)
- Legal review process and typical duration
- Security review/questionnaire requirements
- Contract terms and negotiation process
- Budget cycle and fiscal year considerations

**Qualification Questions**:
- What does your procurement process look like?
- Do you require legal review? How long does it typically take?
- Are there security questionnaires or compliance requirements?
- When does your fiscal year end? Is there budget allocated?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Paper process unknown
- 5-6: General understanding, many unknowns
- 7-8: Process mapped, timeline estimated
- 9-10: All requirements known, timeline confirmed

---

### P - Pain (Identify Pain)
**Definition**: The critical business issues driving the need for change. Pain creates urgency.

**Pain Categories**:
- **Technical Pain**: System limitations, performance issues, integration gaps
- **Business Pain**: Lost revenue, excessive costs, competitive disadvantage
- **Personal Pain**: Career risk, workload, frustration

**Intensity Indicators**:
- LOW: Problem acknowledged but tolerable
- MEDIUM: Active looking for solutions
- HIGH: Urgent need, timeline pressure, executive attention

**Qualification Questions**:
- What prompted you to look at this now?
- What happens if you don't solve this problem?
- How is this impacting your business/team today?
- What have you tried before? Why didn't it work?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: No clear pain identified, "nice to have"
- 5-6: Pain acknowledged but not urgent
- 7-8: Clear pain with business impact quantified
- 9-10: Urgent pain with executive sponsorship

---

### I - Implicate Pain / Intensity
**Definition**: The broader consequences of the pain if left unaddressed. Connects individual pain to organizational impact.

**Implication Levels**:
- Individual: Personal productivity, job satisfaction
- Team: Department efficiency, morale
- Organization: Revenue, competitive position, strategic goals
- External: Customer satisfaction, market share

**Qualification Questions**:
- What happens to the business if this isn't solved?
- How does this problem affect other teams/departments?
- What's the cost of doing nothing?
- How does this connect to your strategic priorities?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Pain isolated, no broader implications discussed
- 5-6: Some implications noted, not quantified
- 7-8: Clear organizational impact understood
- 9-10: Pain connected to strategic priorities with urgency

---

### C - Champion
**Definition**: An internal advocate with power and influence who is actively selling on your behalf.

**Champion Characteristics**:
- Has personal win tied to your success
- Has access to Economic Buyer
- Has influence with key stakeholders
- Willing to fight for you internally
- Shares competitive information

**Testing the Champion**:
- Will they arrange access to Economic Buyer?
- Will they share internal documents/org charts?
- Will they coach you on competition and politics?
- Will they advocate in meetings you're not in?

**Qualification Questions**:
- Why is this important to you personally?
- Can you help me understand the internal dynamics?
- Who might oppose this initiative?
- Can you introduce me to [Economic Buyer]?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: No champion identified
- 5-6: Potential champion, not tested
- 7-8: Confirmed champion, actively helping
- 9-10: Champion with EB access, proven advocacy

---

### C - Competition
**Definition**: All alternatives the prospect is considering, including status quo, internal builds, and other vendors.

**Competition Types**:
- **Status Quo**: Doing nothing, current process
- **Internal**: Build vs. buy, IT projects
- **Direct**: Competing vendors
- **Indirect**: Alternative approaches to solving the problem

**Qualification Questions**:
- What other solutions are you evaluating?
- Have you considered building this internally?
- What happens if you decide to do nothing?
- What would make you choose a competitor over us?

**Scoring Guide**:
- 1-4: Competition unknown
- 5-6: Know competitors, unclear positioning
- 7-8: Competition mapped, we're differentiated
- 9-10: Clear competitive advantage, preferred vendor

---

## Scoring Summary

| Score | Status | Color | Meaning |
|-------|--------|-------|---------|
| 1-4 | Critical Gap | RED | Blocks deal progression |
| 5-6 | Needs Work | YELLOW | Partial information, risks |
| 7-8 | Good | GREEN | Strong position, minor gaps |
| 9-10 | Excellent | GREEN | Fully qualified |

## Deal Health Assessment

| Total Score | Assessment |
|-------------|------------|
| 0-32 (0-40%) | Early stage / At risk |
| 33-48 (41-60%) | Developing, significant gaps |
| 49-64 (61-80%) | Qualified, working key gaps |
| 65-80 (81-100%) | Well qualified, high confidence |

## Maturity Considerations

Adjust expectations based on deal stage:
- **Discovery Call**: Expect scores 3-5 on most elements
- **2-3 Calls**: Should have 5-7 on Pain, Champion, Decision Criteria
- **Late Stage**: Need 7+ on Economic Buyer, Decision Process, Paper Process
